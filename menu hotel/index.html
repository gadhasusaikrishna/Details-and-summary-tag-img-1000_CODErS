MENU-CARD

Indian-Style
  • Samber-Rice

    • Sambar rice is a comforting South Indian dish made by combining cooked rice with flavorful sambar, a spicy and tangy lentil-based vegetable stew. It’s typically seasoned with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and a blend of aromatic spices. Rich in taste and wholesome in nutrition, sambar rice is often enjoyed with a dollop of ghee and served alongside papad or pickle for a satisfying meal.

  • Dal-Rice

    • Dal rice is a simple and wholesome Indian comfort food made by mixing cooked lentils (dal) with steamed rice. Lightly seasoned with spices like cumin, turmeric, and sometimes garlic or ghee, it's both nourishing and easy to digest. Loved for its warmth and simplicity, dal rice is a go-to meal for many, offering both taste and nutrition in every bite.

  • Kichidi-Rice

    • Khichdi rice is a comforting and nutritious Indian dish made by cooking rice and lentils together with mild spices. Often seasoned with cumin, ginger, and turmeric, it’s soft, easy to digest, and perfect for all age groups. Sometimes vegetables are added for extra flavor and nutrition. Khichdi is often served with ghee, pickle, or curd, making it a soothing and satisfying meal.

American-Style
  • Roasted-Turkey

    • Roasted turkey is a classic dish, especially popular during festive occasions like Thanksgiving. It’s known for its golden, crispy skin and juicy, flavorful meat. Often seasoned with herbs, butter, and spices, the turkey is slow-roasted to perfection, filling the kitchen with a mouthwatering aroma. Served with sides like stuffing, cranberry sauce, and gravy, it's a comforting and hearty centerpiece for any special meal.

  • Southern fried chicken

    • Southern fried chicken is a crispy, flavorful dish made by marinating chicken in buttermilk, seasoning it with a blend of spices, then deep-frying it to golden perfection. It's known for its crunchy coating and juicy inside. Often paired with waffles in the iconic "chicken and waffles" combo, this sweet and savory dish brings together the richness of fried chicken with the soft, syrup-drizzled goodness of waffles—a true Southern comfort classic.

  • Crab-Cake

    • Crab cakes are savory patties made primarily from lump crab meat, mixed with breadcrumbs, eggs, and seasonings like mustard, herbs, and a hint of lemon. They're typically pan-fried or baked until golden brown on the outside and tender on the inside. A popular coastal delicacy, especially in Maryland, crab cakes are often served with tartar sauce or a zesty remoulade and make for a delicious appetizer or light main course.

  • Cray-Fish

    • Crayfish, also known as crawfish or crawdads, are small, freshwater crustaceans that resemble tiny lobsters. Popular in Southern cuisine, especially in Louisiana, they are often boiled with spices, corn, and potatoes in a flavorful seafood boil. Crayfish have a sweet, tender meat that's typically found in the tail, and they're a key ingredient in dishes like étouffée, gumbo, and jambalaya. Eating them is a hands-on experience—pinch the tail, suck the head, and enjoy the bold, spicy flavors!

  • LobstaR-Coal

    • Lobster dishes are luxurious and flavorful, often featuring tender, sweet lobster meat as the star ingredient. One classic preparation is boiled or steamed whole lobster, served with melted butter and lemon. Other popular dishes include lobster bisque—a creamy, rich soup—and lobster rolls, where chilled lobster meat is mixed with mayo or butter and served in a toasted bun. Whether grilled, baked, or added to pasta, lobster adds a touch of elegance and indulgence to any meal.

Afgani-Style
  • Kabuli Pulao

    • Kabuli Pulao a rice dish with lamb, carrots, and raisins, Mantu dumplings, and Bolani (stuffed flatbread), along with various kebabs, curries, and desserts.

  • Afghan kofta

    • Afghan Kofta is a flavorful meatball dish made from ground meat (usually beef or lamb) mixed with onions, garlic, herbs, and spices. The koftas are shaped into balls and simmered in a rich, spiced tomato-based gravy. It's often served with rice or flatbreads like naan, making for a hearty and comforting meal with bold flavors typical of Afghan cuisine.

  • Bhind-FRY

    • **Afghan Bhindi** is a flavorful okra dish from Afghan cuisine, known for its simple yet aromatic preparation. Fresh okra is sautéed with onions, tomatoes, garlic, and a blend of mild spices, allowing the natural flavor of the vegetable to shine through. Unlike some spicier Indian versions, Afghan Bhindi tends to be more subtly seasoned, focusing on freshness and texture. It’s often served with naan or rice and pairs well with yogurt-based sides for a wholesome meal.
